1. Design and Development
Client Briefing: The process begins with understanding the client's needs, including the design, fabric, style, and quantity.
Pattern Making: Developing patterns for the garment to ensure proper fit and specifications.
Sampling (if needed): Creating a prototype or sample for approval and revisions, if the client requires it.
2. Sourcing Materials
Fabric and Supplier Selection: Sourcing fabrics, threads, zippers, buttons, and labels based on the design, while establishing partnerships with suppliers for quality and timely delivery.
3. Production Planning
Order Confirmation: Client confirms the order with details like quantity, fabric, and timeline.
Production Schedule: Creating a timeline and allocating resources, including labor and machinery.
4. Sewing and Finishing
Fabric Cutting and Sewing: Cutting fabric pieces accurately and sewing them together in sequence. Quality control is conducted during this stage.
Ironing and Pressing: Ensuring garments maintain the correct shape and appearance.
Final Quality Check: Inspecting the garments for defects and ensuring they meet design specifications.
5. Packaging and Shipping
Tagging, Labeling, and Packing: Garments are tagged, labeled, and packed as per client requirements.
Shipping: Garments are shipped to the client or distributor according to agreed delivery terms.
